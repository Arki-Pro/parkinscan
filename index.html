01 · The Challenge

A diagnostic gap
affecting millions.

Parkinson's disease remains critically under-diagnosed in India — not because of ignorance, but because of access.

Problem

Specialist access is a luxury

India has an estimated 5.8 million Parkinson's patients — a burden projected to double by 2040. Neurological screening is confined to tertiary care centres in urban India, accessible only to a fraction of patients. The majority receive diagnosis only at advanced motor impairment stages, when therapeutic intervention is significantly less effective.

Solution

ParkScreen brings screening to the field

A low-cost wearable platform integrating multimodal biosignal sensing — acoustic voice dysphonia, kinematic motor tremor, and gait analysis — with a dual-model edge-AI ensemble. Deployable by ASHA workers and paramedics without specialist training. Zero chemical waste. No cloud dependency. Manufactured at ₹1,500.

02 · Clinical Biomarkers

22 validated
biological signals.

Every biomarker is grounded in peer-reviewed clinical literature — Jitter and Shimmer thresholds from Little et al. (2007), motor parameters aligned to UPDRS Part 3.

F₀
Fundamental Frequency
Tracks vocal pitch monotonicity — low variance indicates severe laryngeal muscle rigidity from dopaminergic neuron degeneration.
Jitter
Relative Frequency Perturbation
Measures cycle-to-cycle frequency variation in vocal fold oscillation.
Pathological threshold > 1.04%
Shimmer
Local Amplitude Perturbation
Measures cycle-to-cycle amplitude degradation in the vocal signal.
Clinical degradation > 3.81 dB
HNR
Harmonic-to-Noise Ratio
Quantifies vocal hoarseness — reflects glottal closure completeness.
Drop below 20 dB → pathological
NHR
Noise-to-Harmonics Ratio
Tracks noise component dominance over structural harmonics. Inverse of HNR — increases with dysphonia severity.
PPE
Pitch Period Entropy
Logarithmic measure of pitch coefficient variation — designed to resist natural pitch drift artifacts.
RPDE
Recurrence Period Density Entropy
Quantifies vocal signal periodicity anomalies. Outputs a chaos-index score between 0 and 1.
DFA
Detrended Fluctuation Analysis
Evaluates long-range statistical self-affinity and fractal scaling properties of the speech signal.
MFCCs
Mel-Frequency Cepstral Coefficients
Spatial vectors mapping the mathematical shape envelope of the vocal tract.
Dimensionality: 13–26 coefficients
TDF
Tremor Dominant Frequency
PSD peak isolated from IMU signal for resting tremor identification.
Clinical band: 4–6 Hz
TAM
Tremor Amplitude Magnitude
Double integral of IMU linear acceleration — real-time kinetic displacement in millimetres.
SE
Spectral Entropy (IMU)
Measures frequency distribution randomness — a heavy drop isolates pure rhythmic resting tremor from noise.
BI
Bradykinesia Index
Amplitude decay over consecutive movement cycles using exponential decay model.
A(t) = A₀ · e^(−λt)
RI
Rigidity Index (Damping Ratio)
Mechanical stiffness coefficient derived from resistance torque and angular velocity analysis.
GC
Gait Cadence
Step rate tracking — significant deviations signal locomotion impairment.
Normal: 100–115 steps/min
STV
Step Time Variability
Standard deviation (σ) of consecutive heel-strike intervals — maps locomotor coordination failure.
FoG
Freeze of Gait Ratio
Spectral energy ratio between freeze band and normal locomotor band.
Freeze: 3–8 Hz · Locomotor: 0.5–3 Hz
AMS
Adaptive Modality Selection
Graceful fallback routine. If environmental audio SNR is compromised, system actively drops voice vectors and infers strictly from kinematic pathways.
Active Dynamic Weighting
EES
Battery-Efficient Edge Scheduling
Strict hardware scheduling logic. Peripherals execute inside restricted capture loops; mic and camera sleep until evaluation trigger.
Low Power Cycle Control
PBM
Personalized Baseline Mode
Enables local longitudinal delta evaluation by scaling population norm metrics against the patient's individual running historical average.
Intra-subject Normalized
AWL
Analysis Window Length
Fixed processing matrix size with continuous horizontal overlap.
Window: 25 ms · Overlap: 10 ms
ELB
Edge Latency Budget
Real-time on-device binary classification inference execution cap.
Max latency: < 200–500 ms
03 · ML Architecture

Dual-model ensemble
with temporal fusion.

Two fundamentally different classifiers run in parallel on the same feature vector. Their outputs collapse into a single clinically interpretable risk score, smoothed across time.

Input
22 Feature Vector
Acoustic + IMU biomarkers extracted per 25ms window
Model 1
Logistic Regression
Probabilistic, clinically interpretable. Outputs P_LR ∈ [0,1]
Model 2
Linear SVM
Margin-based. Validated on Oxford dataset ~91% accuracy. Outputs P_SVM ∈ [0,1]
Fusion
Weighted Collapse
α optimised on validation cohort. Rolling temporal buffer smoothing
Output
Risk Score
Low / Moderate / High — aligned with UPDRS Part 3
Fusion Formula
Risk_Score = α · P_LR + (1 − α) · P_SVM
where α is empirically optimised on the Indian patient validation cohort
Bradykinesia Decay Model
A(t) = A₀ · e^(−λt)
Amplitude decay over consecutive movement cycles — exponential model

05 · Evaluation & Performance Metrics

Clinically structured.
Validation-ready.

All metrics evaluate screening-level clinical utility and signal consistency — not diagnostic accuracy. ParkScreen is a decision-support tool, not a diagnostic replacement.

r
Clinical Correlation (Spearman/Pearson)
Biomedical validation hook. Tracks correlation between composite biomarker indicators (TSI, MSI, VSI) and golden-standard clinical UPDRS Part III motor scores.
Target: r > 0.6 strong · 0.4–0.6 early prototype
ROC-AUC
Classification Performance
Measures receiver operating characteristic curves across risk bands. Screening logic aggressively prioritizes Sensitivity (Recall) to completely suppress false negatives.
Target: AUC > 0.75 for pilot phase
ICC
Test-Retest Reliability
Intraclass Correlation Coefficient measures cross-session output stability when identical human subjects are recorded multiple times under variable edge environments.
Target: ICC > 0.75 → Good Stability
CV%
Intra-Subject Variability
Coefficient of variation tracking consistency over tight repeated intervals. Lower values verify sensor reliability and isolate structural symptoms from noise.
Lower CV% = Higher Reliability
DQI
Data Quality Index (SNR / Artifact)
Signal quality gate before running edge inference. Calculates raw Signal-to-Noise Ratio for voice/IMU lines and calculates the precise percentage of unusable recordings.
Rejects Garbage Inputs Automatically
FRM
Feature Robustness (Fisher Score)
Evaluates cross-subject variance against within-subject variance ratios. Ensures extracted Parkinson's features remain generalizable and are not dataset-specific quirks.
Fisher Score / Variance Ratio Validation
RAM / PWR
On-Device Edge MicroML Bounds
Hardware constraints verification on the ESP32 chip. Hard target restrictions ensure continuous workflow execution under battery deployment scenarios.
Model < 100 KB · Latency < 200–500ms
CEM
Cost Efficiency Scale Factor
Calculates macro financial scalability. Targets low bill-of-materials and driving transactional screening session operation costs down near absolute zero.
Target Production < ₹800–₹1,200
CUM
Clinical Utility & Variance Reduction
Tracks reduction in false-negative screenings while evaluating the percentage drop in score assessment variance across independent, non-expert field operators.
Screening Time < 5 minutes / Patient
OPS
Rural Deployment Success Rate
Quantifies system usability score (Likert Scale) and verification performance trends across offline, low-resource operational configurations in rural environments.
Target: 100% Core Grid-Independent
CAO / FDR
Confidence-Aware False Diagnosis Avoidance
Pairs every output risk index with a strict confidence tier. Flags uncertain low-confidence parameters explicitly to track human neurologist override rates.
Humility First — Override Friendly Architecture
XAI
Explainable Biomarker Reporting
Breaks open the black box. Generates natural biomarker breakdown reports explaining classifications (e.g., "Elevated tremor frequency isolated").
Per-Biomarker Impact Breakdown
LT
Longitudinal Trajectory Tracking
Saves chronological TSI, MSI, and VSI score arrays locally to monitor progression curves. Clinicians treat trend lines, not static points.
Multi-Visit Delta Analytics
Key Clinical Positioning (DBT REVIEW REQUIREMENT)
“All metrics are designed to evaluate screening-level clinical utility and signal consistency, not diagnostic accuracy.”
ParkScreen flags abnormalities to offload overloaded medical pipelines — it does not replace expert definitive diagnosis.
